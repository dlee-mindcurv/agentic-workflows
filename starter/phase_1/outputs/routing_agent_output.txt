The Roman Kingdom was the earliest period of Roman history, followed by the Roman Republic, which lasted from 509 BC to 27 BC. During the Republic, Rome expanded its territory through conquest and established a republican form of government with a Senate and elected officials.

The decline of the Roman Empire began in the 3rd century AD due to internal strife, economic instability, and invasions by barbarian tribes. In 476 AD, the last Roman Emperor in the West was deposed, marking the end of the Western Roman Empire.

Despite the fall of the Western Roman Empire, the Eastern Roman Empire, also known as the Byzantine Empire, continued to thrive until the 15th century. Rome remained an important city throughout the Middle Ages and Renaissance, eventually becoming the capital of a unified Italy in 1871.
